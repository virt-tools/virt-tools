Rail Fence Cipher

Encrypt and decrypt with the Rail Fence cipher (also known as the zigzag cipher), a form of transposition cipher. It writes the message in a zigzag pattern across a fixed number of "rails" (rows) and then reads them off row by row. This scrambles the order of letters without changing their identities. Everything runs locally in your browser.

Zigzag Visualization

The math. The Rail Fence cipher is a transposition cipher. To encrypt, you write the message in a zigzag across $N$ rails. For example, with 3 rails, "HELLO" would be:
Row 1: H . . . O
Row 2: . E . L .
Row 3: . . L . .
The resulting ciphertext is the concatenation of the rows: "HOEL L". To decrypt, you must know the number of rails, mark the zigzag positions, and fill them with the ciphertext letters row by row before reading the zigzag back. Pairs with the Atbash, Caesar, and Vigenère ciphers. Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
