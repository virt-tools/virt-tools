Gear Ratio & RPM Calculator

Compute the ratio between two meshing gears (or a belt-and-pulley drive) and what it does to speed and torque. Enter the tooth counts (or pulley diameters) of the driver and driven members plus an input RPM and torque to get the gear ratio, output RPM, and output torque with an efficiency loss; or solve for the missing member that produces a target ratio. Pairs with the Resistor and Capacitor series/parallel tools for mechanical reduction. Everything runs locally in your browser.

Ratio = driven / driver. A ratio greater than 1 is a speed reducer: the output turns slower but with more torque. Output RPM = input RPM × driver / driven. Output torque = input torque × driven / driver × efficiency. Power is conserved (minus losses): torque rises as speed falls. For belt drives use pulley diameters the same way. Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
