Hooke's law F = k·x: a spring exerts a restoring force proportional to its displacement from equilibrium (k is the stiffness in N/m). The elastic potential energy stored is U = ½·k·x². A mass m on an ideal spring (massless, no friction) oscillates in simple harmonic motion with angular frequency ω = √(k/m), period T = 2π·√(m/k), and frequency f = 1/T. For an amplitude A the maximum speed is v_max = A·ω and the maximum acceleration is a_max = A·ω². Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
