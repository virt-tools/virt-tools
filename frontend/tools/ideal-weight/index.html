Ideal Body Weight Calculator

Estimate ideal body weight (IBW) from height and sex using four published clinical formulas — Hamwi (1964), Devine (1974), Robinson (1983), and Miller (1983) — and report the range and average. Formulas compute a target weight for a given height (intended for adults, originally based on actuarial life-insurance data), with separate constants for men and women and a per-inch correction above 5 ft (152 cm). Enter height in centimetres or feet/inches. The results are population averages, not individual medical targets — muscle mass, frame size, and body composition all shift a healthy weight. Everything runs locally in your browser.

Formulas give IBW in kg for a height of at least 5 ft (60 in / 152 cm); the per-inch correction is applied for heights above (and, by convention, below) that reference. Devine: men 50 + 2.3×(in−60), women 45.5 + 2.3×(in−60). Robinson: men 52 + 1.9×(in−60), women 49 + 1.7×(in−60). Miller: men 56.2 + 1.41×(in−60), women 53.1 + 1.36×(in−60). Hamwi: men 48 + 2.7×(in−60), women 45.5 + 2.2×(in−60). Range and average across the four formulas. Runs locally.
