Depth of Field Calculator

Find the hyperfocal distance and the near and far limits of acceptable focus for a camera lens. Enter the focal length, aperture (f-number), and subject distance, then choose a sensor or circle of confusion. Reports the hyperfocal distance, the near and far focus distances, the total depth of field, and how much of it lies in front of and behind the subject. Everything runs locally in your browser.

Circle of confusion (sensor)

Hyperfocal H = f²/(N·c) + f. Near limit Dn = s(H−f)/(H + s − 2f); far limit Df = s(H−f)/(H − s), taken as infinity when the subject is at or beyond the hyperfocal distance. Distances and the focal length are in the same unit internally (mm); the circle of confusion is in mm. Standard sensor CoC values are presets; enter a custom one for other formats or a personal sharpness standard. Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
