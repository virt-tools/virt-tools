Scytale Cipher

Encrypt and decrypt with the Scytale cipher, an ancient Greek transposition cipher. The message is written on a strip of parchment wrapped around a rod (the scytale) of a specific diameter. The ciphertext is read by unwrapping the strip, and decryption requires a rod of the exact same diameter to bring the letters back into alignment. Everything runs locally in your browser.

The math. The Scytale cipher is a transposition cipher that operates similarly to a Rail Fence cipher but without the zigzag. It writes the text in a grid with a fixed number of columns (the diameter). The plaintext is written row-by-row, and the ciphertext is read column-by-column. For a message of length $L$ and a rod of diameter $D$, the ciphertext is formed by taking every $D$-th letter starting from the first, then every $D$-th starting from the second, and so on. Pairs with the Rail Fence, Caesar, and Vigenère cipher tools. Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
