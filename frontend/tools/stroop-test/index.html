Stroop Test

Test your selective attention and cognitive interference. A color word appears printed in a possibly different ink color — pick the button whose label matches the ink color, not the word. When the word and ink match (congruent) it's easy; when they don't (incongruent) your reading reflex fights your color judgment. The measured gap in reaction time is the Stroop effect. Everything runs locally in your browser.

Each trial: a color word (e.g. "RED") appears in some ink color. The six buttons below are labeled with color names in neutral text — click the one whose label names the ink color you see. The classic finding: incongruent trials (word and ink differ) take ~100–200 ms longer and produce more errors than congruent trials, because reading is automatic and interferes with naming the color. The reported Stroop effect is the mean RT gap. Runs locally — no data leaves your browser.
