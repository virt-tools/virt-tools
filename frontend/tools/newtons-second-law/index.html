Newton's second law F = m·a (1 newton accelerates 1 kg by 1 m/s²). Weight is the gravitational force on a mass, W = m·g; it varies with local gravity (Earth ≈ 9.80665, Moon ≈ 1.62, Mars ≈ 3.71, Jupiter ≈ 24.79 m/s²). Momentum p = m·v (kg·m/s) is conserved for isolated systems. Kinetic energy KE = ½·m·v² (joules). Unit conversions: 1 lb = 0.45359237 kg; 1 oz = 28.3495 g; 1 lbf ≈ 4.44822 N; 1 kgf = 9.80665 N; 1 dyn = 10 µN; 1 g (accel) = 9.80665 m/s²; 1 mph = 0.44704 m/s; 1 km/h = 0.277778 m/s; 1 ft/s = 0.3048 m/s. Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
