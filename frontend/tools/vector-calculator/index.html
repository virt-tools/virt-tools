Operations

A vector is a quantity with direction and magnitude. Magnitude |A| = √(x²+y²+z²) is its length. The dot product A·B is a scalar (zero when the vectors are perpendicular). The cross product A×B is a vector perpendicular to both (3D); its magnitude is the area of the parallelogram they span. The angle between vectors is θ = acos(A·B / (|A||B|)). The scalar projection of A onto B is A·B/|B|; the vector projection is (A·B/B·B)·B. The unit vector Â = A/|A| keeps the direction with length 1. Pairs with the Trigonometry and Triangle Solver tools. Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
