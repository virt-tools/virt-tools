Atbash Cipher

Encrypt and decrypt with the Atbash cipher, a monoalphabetic substitution cipher originally used to encrypt the Hebrew alphabet. It works by reversing the alphabet: the first letter (A) is replaced by the last (Z), the second (B) by the second-to-last (Y), and so on. Because it is its own inverse, the same process is used for both encryption and decryption. Everything runs locally in your browser.

The math. The Atbash cipher is a simple mapping where each letter $L$ at position $i$ (0-indexed) in the alphabet is replaced by the letter at position $(25 - i)$. For example, A (0) becomes Z (25), and B (1) becomes Y (24). It is a self-reciprocal cipher: applying it twice restores the original text. Pairs with the Caesar, Vigenère, and Classical Ciphers tools. Everything runs locally — nothing leaves your browser.
