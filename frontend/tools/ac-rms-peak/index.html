AC RMS / Peak Converter

Convert between the amplitude measures of an AC waveform. For a sine wave the RMS (root-mean-square) value is Vrms = Vpk/√2 ≈ 0.707·Vpk, the peak-to-peak is Vpp = 2·Vpk, and the full-wave rectified average (what an analog moving-coil meter reads) is 2·Vpk/π ≈ 0.637·Vpk. The ratio of peak to RMS is the crest factor (√2 ≈ 1.414 for a sine) and the ratio of RMS to average is the form factor (π/(2√2) ≈ 1.11). Different waveforms have different factors: a square wave's RMS equals its peak (crest factor 1), a triangle's RMS is Vpk/√3. Enter any one of peak / RMS / peak-to-peak and pick a waveform to get the others, optionally add a DC offset (combined RMS = √(Vdc² + Vac,rms²)), or paste a list of sample values to compute the RMS, mean, and crest factor directly from the general definition √(mean(v²)). Everything runs locally in your browser.

Waveform reference

The math. The RMS value of a waveform is the equivalent DC that delivers the same average power to a resistor: Vrms = √( (1/T)·∫₀ᵀ v(t)² dt ) — square the signal, average over a cycle, take the square root. For a sine v = Vpk·sin(ωt) this integrates to Vpk/√2. The peak-to-peak is simply 2·Vpk. The full-wave rectified average is (1/T)·∫|v(t)|dt = 2·Vpk/π for a sine; analog panel meters calibrated in RMS actually rectify and average, then assume a sine's form factor (RMS/average = π/(2√2) ≈ 1.11) — so they're only accurate for sines. The crest factor peak/RMS matters for measurement instruments: a true-RMS meter must handle high crest factors (e.g. impulsive currents) without clipping. A square wave has crest factor 1 (RMS = peak), a triangle Vpk/√3 (crest factor √3), a half-wave rectified sine has RMS Vpk/2 and average Vpk/π. When a DC offset is present the total RMS combines in quadrature: Vrms,total = √(Vdc² + Vac,rms². The samples tool applies the definition directly — useful for arbitrary or measured waveforms.
